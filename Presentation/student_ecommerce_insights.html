üìã Abstract

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of student online shopping behaviour using data mining, statistical analysis, and machine learning techniques. The study focuses on understanding key factors influencing students' e-commerce preferences and spending patterns.

Key Highlights

  • Dataset collected through survey responses (200 students)
  • Analysis performed using R on Google Colab
  • Applied EDA, Association Rule Mining, and Random Forest
  • Revealed that product category and platform are most influential factors
  • Insights support targeted strategies and personalized recommendations

üéØ Motivation & Objectives

Why This Project?
Online shopping has become integral to student life, driven by convenience, discounts, and social media influence. Students represent a significant consumer segment with unique preferences and evolving shopping habits.

Project Objectives

1. Identify Key Factors

Analyze factors influencing students' online shopping behavior

2. Discover Patterns

Uncover association patterns using data mining techniques

3. Build Predictive Models

Develop models to identify high-spending customers

4. Actionable Insights

Bridge gap between raw data and business strategies
